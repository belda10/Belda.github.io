THOR

Thor, the God of Thunder, is a superhero from Marvel Comics, created by writer Stan Lee, scripter Larry Lieber, and artist Jack Kirby. He first appeared in "Journey into Mystery" #83 in 1962. Thor is based on the Norse mythological deity of the same name.

As the son of Odin, the All-Father, Thor possesses incredible strength, durability, and the ability to wield lightning. He wields Mjolnir, a powerful enchanted hammer that allows him to fly and manipulate weather. Thor's character embodies themes of heroism, honor, and redemption as he learns to balance his divine responsibilities with his desire to protect Earth.

In the Marvel Cinematic Universe, Thor is portrayed by Chris Hemsworth and has appeared in several films, including the "Thor" series and the "Avengers" series. His journey explores his growth from a brash warrior to a wise leader, facing challenges such as the destruction of Asgard and the threat of powerful foes like Hela and Thanos.
